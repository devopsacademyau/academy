    I just need to “borrow” 2 bits from the host part in order to create 4 subnets.

    • 10.0.0.0/16 

    /16 + 2 = /18,  32bits – 18 = 14,  2^14 = 16.384 – 5 = 16,379 usable hosts IPs for each

    10.0.0.0 IP in binary:   00001010.00000000.00000000.00000000
    /18 :                    11111111.11111111.11000000.00000000 = 255.255.192.0 new subnet mask in decimals   
    End of the range:        00000000.00000000.00111111.11111111 = 0.0.63.255 
    (by changing the subnet masks 0’s for 1’s and vice versa)

    Range Networks                  Range of usable hosts 
    10.0.0.0 – 10.0.63.255          10.0.0.4 – 10.0.63.254                    
    10.0.64.0 - 10.0.127.255        10.0.64.4 - 10.0.127.254
    10.0.128.0 - 10.0.191.255       10.0.128.4 - 10.0.191.254
    10.0.192.0 - 10.0.255.255       10.0.192.4 - 10.0.255.254


    • 192.168.0.0/24

    /24 + 2 = /26  , 32bits – 26 = 6  , 2^6= 64 - 5 = 59 usable host IPs for each

    192.168.0.0 IP in binary:  11000000.10101000.00000000.00000000
    /26 :                      11111111.11111111.11111111.11000000 = 255.255.255.192 new subnet mask
    End of range =             00000000.00000000.00000000.00111111 = 0.0.0.63
